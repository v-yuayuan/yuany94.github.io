Hi, there! My first name is Yuan [源]. And, surprisingly my last name is also Yuan [袁]. I was lucky enough to be selected in the first batch of the Social Engineering Systems Ph.D. program in Institute for Data, Systems, and Society (IDSS) at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. I have received multidisciplinary training in my program, including machine learning, statistics, economics, and other social sciences. Now I am a third-year Ph.D. student, who is broadly interested in analyzing human behavior big data, i.e., computational social science. I find myself especially excited about causal inference, social network analysis, and the application of machine learning to social sciences. I am a research assistant in MIT Media Lab, advised by Prof. Sandy Pentland.

Publications

An Interpretable Approach for Social Network Formation Among Heterogeneous Agents

Nature Communications, forthcoming

Authors: Yuan Yuan, Ahmad Alabdulkareem, Alex Pentland

Here we integrate the methods from game theory, machine learning (node embedding), and agent-based modeling to establish a network formation model. We test our model with large-scale empirical networks.

Online Red Packets: A Large-Scale Natural Experiment of Pay-It-Forward Reciprocity

Working paper

Authors: Yuan Yuan, Tracy Xiao Liu, Chenhao Tan, Jie Tang, Alex Pentland

We utilize the randomness of the observational data of online red packets to identify the causal relationship of receiving gifts and pay-it-forward reciprocity.

About My Name

I know my name is confusing but let me clarify here. My family name is Yuan [袁] which I do not actually know what it means. The most famous person in Chinese history with the same family name is Shikai Yuan: he was the last emperor in the history of China, but only for 83 days. My given name is Yuan [源], which means the source of a river or stream. My family name and given name are just somehow have the same pronunciation. In the language system of Chinese, many characters (words) have the same pronunciation. How to pronounce Yuan (either [源] or [袁]): see 2:52.
